Logistics

Getting to Udupi

November is peak holiday season — flights and trains book up fast. We strongly recommend booking your travel at least 6–8 weeks in advance.

✈️

By Air

The nearest airport is Mangalore International Airport (IXE), about 60 km from Udupi. Direct flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, and Hyderabad. From Mangalore, take a cab or bus to Udupi (approx 1–1.5 hrs).

🚂

By Train

Udupi Railway Station is on the Konkan Railway line — one of India's most scenic routes. Direct trains from Mumbai (Matsyagandha Express) and connections from Bangalore and Mangalore. Book early — sleeper and AC classes fill up months ahead in November.

🚗

By Road

From Bangalore: ~5–6 hrs (360 km via NH75). From Mangalore: ~1 hr (60 km). The drive is beautiful — coastal ghats, greenery, and sea views. Cab aggregators like Ola/Uber work from Mangalore airport.

Local Transport

Getting Around Udupi

Udupi is compact and easy to navigate once you're here. Here's how locals get around.

🛵

Scooter / Bike Rental

The best way to explore at your own pace. Rentals are cheap (₹300–500/day) and widely available near the town centre. Useful for day trips to nearby beaches and temples.

🚕

Autos & Cabs

Ola and Uber operate in Udupi. Local auto-rickshaws are plentiful for short hops. For longer day trips (Murudeshwar, Jog Falls), hire a cab for the day — negotiate a fixed rate upfront.

🚌

KSRTC Buses

State buses connect Udupi to Mangalore, Manipal, and nearby towns. Cheap and frequent but not the most comfortable. Good fallback if you're comfortable with it.

Food

Places to Eat

Udupi is the birthplace of some of India's most iconic vegetarian food — but the coastal non-veg here is equally legendary. Eat everything.

🥗 Vegetarian

Hotel Woodlands Must Try
The gold standard for Udupi meals — thali, idli, vada, dosas. An institution since 1938. Try the Udupi special thali.
Mitra Samaj
Famous for its filter coffee and soft idlis. A local breakfast staple — get there before 9am.
Diana Hotel
Old-school Udupi restaurant. Try the set dosa and the rava idli.
Hanchina Mane
Home-style Udupi cuisine in a traditional setting. Great for lunch.

🍖 Non-Vegetarian

Kediyoor Hotel Must Try
Legendary for coastal Tulu food — fish curry, crab, kane (ladyfish) fry. The kind of place locals go on birthdays.
Hotel Sai Ram Comforts
Excellent Mangalorean seafood. Try the prawn ghee roast and kori rotti.
SR Pai Hotel
Simple, no-frills, outstanding fish fry. Popular with locals — always a good sign.
Hotel Udupi Krishna Bhavan
Also does good non-veg coastal dishes alongside their veg menu.

Places to Visit

Plan a few extra days — there's a lot to see. Udupi itself is small, but the surrounding region is spectacular.

In Udupi

Sri Krishna Temple

One of the most sacred Vaishnava temples in India. The unique window darshan (Kanakana Kindi) is unlike any temple experience. Go early morning.

Town centre · 10 min from most hotels
Nearby · 6 km

Malpe Beach

Udupi's main beach — great for sunsets, water sports, and fresh seafood stalls along the shore. Take a boat to St. Mary's Island from here.

15 min from Udupi
Day Trip · 80 km

Murudeshwar

Home to the world's second-tallest Shiva statue (249 ft). The temple sits on a promontory surrounded by the Arabian Sea on three sides — stunning at sunset.

~2 hrs by road
Day Trip · 100 km

Gokarna

A sacred temple town with some of Goa's better beach alternatives. Om Beach, Half Moon Beach. More spiritual and laid-back than Goa.

~2.5 hrs by road
Day Trip · 250 km

Jog Falls

India's highest plunge waterfall. November is post-monsoon — the falls will be at full force. Dramatic and worth the drive.

~4.5 hrs by road
Extended Trip · 250 km

Coorg

Karnataka's hill country — coffee estates, misty valleys, river rafting at Dubare. Great if you want 2–3 extra days in nature before the wedding.

~5 hrs by road

Shopping

Where to Buy Sarees

Karnataka is famous for its silk weaving tradition. Here's where to find the best pieces.

01

Cauvery Handicrafts Emporium — Udupi

Government-run store with a wide range of Karnataka silks, Ilkal sarees, and Mysore silk at fixed, fair prices. No bargaining needed — reliable quality.

02

Mysore (day trip) — Cauvery Arts & Crafts Emporium

If you're extending your trip, Mysore is the home of Mysore Silk. The Cauvery emporium in Mysore has the widest range of authentic Mysore silk sarees. Worth the extra journey.

03

Local Textile Stores — Car Street, Udupi

The Car Street area near Krishna Temple has several traditional textile shops. Good for cotton sarees, Ilkal, and Khadi. More local, more interesting to explore.

04

Mangalore Silk House — Mangalore

If you're coming via Mangalore, this is a well-known store for Kanjeevaram and Mysore silks with good variety. Worth a stop en route.

Coastline

Beaches Near Udupi

The Tulu Nadu coastline is largely unspoiled. Here are the ones worth your time.

Malpe Beach

Udupi's main beach. Lively, good for sunsets and seafood. Starting point for St. Mary's Island boat trips.

6 km from Udupi

St. Mary's Island

Basalt rock formations declared a national geological monument. Take a 15-min boat from Malpe. Unique and beautiful.

Boat from Malpe

Kaup Beach

Quieter than Malpe. Old lighthouse, calm waters. Good for an early morning walk.

12 km from Udupi

Padubidri Beach

Very local, almost no tourists. Long stretch of black sand. Great if you want solitude.

20 km from Udupi

Om Beach, Gokarna

Shaped like the Om symbol. Rocky coves, clearer water than Udupi. Good for a day trip if you go to Gokarna.

100 km · Day trip

Culture

Temples & Culture

Udupi is deeply spiritual — home to the Udupi Peeth, one of the eight monasteries established by Madhvacharya. Here are the places worth visiting.

Sri Krishna Temple, Udupi

The centrepiece. The window darshan (viewing through a window of nine holes) is the tradition started by devotee Kanakadasa. Unmissable.

Kollur Mookambika Temple

One of Karnataka's most revered temples, 80 km from Udupi. Set in the Western Ghats — the drive alone is spectacular.

Murudeshwar Temple

Dramatic coastal setting, giant Shiva statue. More spectacle than spirituality — but absolutely worth it for the views.

Brahmi Durgaparameshwari, Kateel

A temple built on a small island in the Nandini River. Uniquely peaceful. About 50 km from Udupi.

What to Pack

November in Udupi

Post-monsoon, pre-winter — November is honestly one of the nicest months on the Karnataka coast. Here's what to expect.

🌡️

Temperature

25–32°C during the day. Evenings drop to around 22°C. Warm but not oppressive — the sea breeze makes it pleasant.

🌦️

Rain Chance

Post-monsoon — occasional showers but nothing prolonged. Carry a light rain jacket just in case.

👗

What to Wear

Light cottons and linens for daytime. A light layer for evenings. Comfortable footwear for temple visits (you'll be barefoot a lot).

🕶️

Beach Days

Sunny and beautiful. Sunscreen is non-negotiable — the coastal sun is stronger than Delhi. Bring more than you think you need.

Need to Know

Practical Tips

A few things to keep in mind before you travel.

📅

Book Early!

November is peak season — it's Diwali holidays, wedding season, and travel season combined. Flights and trains to Mangalore/Udupi will be packed. Book 6–8 weeks out at minimum.

🗣️

Language

Locals speak Tulu and Kannada. English is understood at hotels and restaurants. A few Kannada words go a long way — "Dhanyavada" (thank you) gets smiles everywhere.

📶

Connectivity

Jio and Airtel work well in Udupi town and most tourist areas. Signal can get patchy in the ghats and some beaches. Download offline maps before you go.

💰

Cash & ATMs

UPI works everywhere in town. For smaller beach stalls and local eateries, carry some cash. ATMs are easy to find in Udupi town centre.

👟

Temple Etiquette

Remove footwear at temples. Dress modestly — shoulders and knees covered. Some temples have a separate dress code for non-Hindus, so check before visiting.

🌊

Beach Safety

The Arabian Sea can have strong currents in November. Swim only in designated areas and heed any flags. St. Mary's Island boat trips run on weather — check timings before going.
